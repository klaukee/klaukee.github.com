SOFTSAFE

Industrial Design (Fall 2010)

Soft Safe is designed specifically for children in transitional living states. These children are often poorly equipped for travelling to and from temporary living places and frequently loose documents needed to attend school in new areas. Without these critical records, many children are turned down from schools and denied public education. To ensure important documentation stays with children during their living transitions, Soft Safe addresses this issue with a unique solution. Simply, the functionality of the travel bag is reliant on the physical attachment of the document holder to complete the product. Children with documents equals children in school. The project was done in collaboration with Danielle Parnes and Maureen Griswold.


Research

Extensive research was conducted before the design process to narrow the focus of the project. The team conducted interviews with three knowledgable and experienced participants who were all involved in various initiatives dealing with children in transitional living. After the series of interviews we discovered the compelling issue that when homeless children travel from various living locations, their legal documents are often lost in transit.

    • Design Process

    When designing the bag we wanted to consider both the lifestyles of our primary users as well as the stigma that is often associated with those in these living situations. After several rapid ideation sessions, the team began refining the final concept for the bag.
