ADRENAJECT

Industrial Design (Fall 2011)

Adrenaject is a personal medical device used to administer a dosage of epinephrine during emergency treatment of severe allergies. When compared to the EpiPen, Adrenaject eliminates 2-3 key steps during the process of use. These steps are crucial time saved during emergency situations. The product also considers a more user-friendly design for both the primary and secondary users.


  • Process of Use

    Research

    Several research methods were conducted in order to explore current user interactions and preferences regarding the design of the EpiPen. I surveyed 36 participants, EpiPen users as well as secondary users, in order to explore areas of opportunity and improvement in the current autoinjector design. After conducting these surveys I researched several autoinjectors and autoinjector concept designs and created a comparitive analysis between the products.

    It was found after comparitive analysis that most of the current concept designs are not mechanically feasible. In addition, many of the current designs on the market are not user-friendly. To ensure my design was relatively feasible I disassembled an EpiPen and studied the internal components.

  • Design Phase

    Ideation for the final design of adrenaject included a mix between hand-sketching and model making. During the model making process user testing was conducted to refine features, interactions and proportions of the design. Additionally, internal mechanics and material processes were considerations during the design phase.
